## 10 States with Specific Components & Cladding Certification Requirements

### 1. **FLORIDA**
**Building Code:** Florida Building Code (FBC) 8th Edition (2023)
**Effective Date:** December 31, 2023
**ASCE 7 Version:** ASCE 7-22
**Certification Requirements:**
- PE/Architect seal required for C&C design
- Product approvals required (FL Number)
- HVHZ zones (Miami-Dade & Broward) have additional requirements

**Special County Requirements (Override ASCE 7):**
- **Miami-Dade County:** 175 mph (Risk Category II), Exposure C minimum
- **Broward County:** 170 mph (Risk Category II), Exposure C minimum
- **Collier County:** Standard FBC requirements

**Key Features:**
- Early adoption of ASCE 7-22
- Product approval system
- NOA (Notice of Acceptance) for HVHZ products
- TAS 201, 202, 203 impact testing requirements
- Wind-borne debris protection required in HVHZ

---

### 2. **TEXAS**
**Building Code:** 2021 International Building Code (IBC) with state amendments
**ASCE 7 Version:** ASCE 7-16 (based on 2021 IBC)
**Certification Requirements:**
- Texas-licensed PE required for windstorm certification in coastal areas
- TDI (Texas Department of Insurance) product evaluations for C&C
- WPI-8 certification for TWIA (Texas Windstorm Insurance Association) coverage

**TDI Product Requirements:**
- Windows, doors, siding, roofing must meet TDI evaluations
- Similar to FL NOA system but voluntary
- Windstorm certification mandatory for coastal areas

---

### 3. **LOUISIANA**
**Building Code:** 2015/2021 International Residential Code (IRC) with state amendments
**ASCE 7 Version:** ASCE 7-10 / ASCE 7-16 (varies by jurisdiction)
**Certification Requirements:**
- PE seal required for high-wind coastal areas
- Wind Zone III designation for coastal parishes (110+ mph)

**Coastal Parishes:** Jefferson, LaFourche, Orleans, Plaquemines, St. Bernard, St. Charles, St. Mary, Terrebonne

---

### 4. **NORTH CAROLINA**
**Building Code:** 2018 North Carolina Residential Code
**ASCE 7 Version:** ASCE 7-16
**Certification Requirements:**

- High Wind Zone provisions (Chapter 45)
- ICC 600 Standard compliance required for 130-150 mph zones

**High Wind Counties:** Carteret, Dare, Hyde (Wind Zone III - 110+ mph)

### 6. **GEORGIA**

**ASCE 7 Version:** ASCE 7-16
**Certification Requirements:**

- Wind-borne debris regions: 130+ mph areas
- Corrosion-resistant hardware required

## Remaining 40 States - General Requirements

### Common Pattern:
Most states adopt International Building Code (IBC) or International Residential Code (IRC) with minimal amendments.

### Typical ASCE 7 Versions by IBC Edition:
- **2024 IBC** → ASCE 7-22
- **2021 IBC** → ASCE 7-16
- **2018 IBC** → ASCE 7-16
- **2015 IBC** → ASCE 7-10

### Standard Requirements:
- PE seal typically required for commercial buildings
- Residential may not require PE seal unless complex
- C&C loads calculated per ASCE 7
- No special product approval systems
- Standard wind speed maps apply

## Key Terminology

**PE Seal:** Professional Engineer stamp/seal required on structural drawings

**NOA:** Notice of Acceptance (Miami-Dade product approval)

**FL Number:** Florida product approval number

**TDI:** Texas Department of Insurance (product evaluations)

**HVHZ:** High Velocity Hurricane Zone (Miami-Dade & Broward Counties)

**TAS:** Testing Application Standard (impact testing protocols)

**Components & Cladding (C&C):** Windows, doors, roofing, siding, etc. - elements that don't contribute to main structural system

**MWFRS:** Main Wind Force Resisting System - structural framing
