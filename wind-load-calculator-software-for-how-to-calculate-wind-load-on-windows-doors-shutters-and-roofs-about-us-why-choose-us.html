24+ Years of Customer-Driven Evolution

2002-2005: Foundation Years

Customer Need: Engineers struggled with complex ASCE 7-05 calculations that required hours of manual work and were prone to errors.

Our Response: We created the first web-based wind load calculator, automating the most tedious aspects while maintaining engineering rigor. Customers told us they needed confidence in the results, so we added detailed calculation reports showing every step.

2006-2008: Learning from Jurisdictions

Customer Need: Building officials across different jurisdictions had varying requirements for documentation and PE seal formats.

Our Response: We introduced customizable report formats, jurisdiction-specific output options, and PE seal integration. When customers in Florida told us about Miami-Dade's unique HVHZ requirements, we built specialized tools to address those needs.

2009-2011: ASCE 7-10 Transition

Customer Need: The industry transition to ASCE 7-10 created confusion. Customers needed to understand what changed, why it mattered, and how to apply new provisions correctly.

Our Response: We didn't just update the formulas—we created educational resources, comparison tools, and detailed commentary explaining the differences. Customers asked for both ASCE 7-05 and ASCE 7-10 in one platform during the transition period, so we provided both.

2012-2015: Expanding for Real-World Complexity

Customer Need: Architects and product manufacturers told us they needed wind load calculations earlier in the design process, before all building details were finalized.

Our Response: We added preliminary calculation modes, product selection tools, and integration options. When window and door manufacturers asked for batch calculation capabilities to help their dealers, we built that functionality.

2016-2018: ASCE 7-16 and Enhanced Accuracy

Customer Need: ASCE 7-16 brought significant methodology changes. Customers needed accuracy, but they also needed speed—projects don't wait for lengthy calculations.

Our Response: We optimized our calculation engine to provide accurate ASCE 7-16 results in seconds, not hours. When jurisdictions asked for specific documentation about wind directionality and topographic factors, we enhanced our reports to provide that transparency.

2019-2021: User Experience Revolution

Customer Need: Customers told us the technical accuracy was excellent, but they wanted the software to be more intuitive, especially for team members who weren't structural engineers.

Our Response: We redesigned the entire user interface based on hundreds of user interviews. We added guided workflows, intelligent defaults, and contextual help. Customers asked for mobile access for site visits, so we made the platform fully responsive.

2022-2024: ASCE 7-22 and Modern Platform

Customer Need: The release of ASCE 7-22 came with new risk-based approaches and updated wind maps. Customers needed to understand the impact on their projects while managing both legacy and new projects.

Our Response: We launched WindLoadCalc.com as a modern, comprehensive platform supporting both ASCE 7-16 and ASCE 7-22. Based on feedback, we added project management features, team collaboration tools, and the Building Intelligence Platform (BIP) to help firms manage multiple projects and compliance requirements efficiently.

2025 and Beyond: Continuous Innovation

Customer Need: The industry continues to evolve with changing climate patterns, updated building codes, and emerging technologies.

Our Response: We remain committed to listening and evolving. Current development focuses on AI-assisted input validation, automated code compliance checking, and integration with popular design software—all requested by our customers.
